## 1. Motivation

Large language models are expensive to serve. A 70B-parameter model at FP16 requires ≈140 GB of VRAM, making single-GPU deployment infeasible. **Post-training quantization (PTQ)** reduces this cost without retraining, but aggressive quantisation—especially below 4-bit weights—degrades model quality.

The core issue is *representational collapse*: quantisation noise is not uniformly harmful. Certain weight subspaces encode disproportionate information; clamping them causes cascading errors in attention heads and MLP projections.

## 2. Method

### 2.1 Fisher Information Proxy

For a pre-trained model with weights $\mathbf{W}$, we define the *saliency score* of weight $w_{ij}$ as the diagonal Fisher information approximated over a small calibration set $\mathcal{D}$:

$$
S_{ij} = \frac{1}{|\mathcal{D}|} \sum_{x \in \mathcal{D}}
  \left(\frac{\partial \mathcal{L}(x; \mathbf{W})}{\partial w_{ij}}\right)^{\!2}
$$

High-saliency weights are *preserved* in higher precision; the remainder are quantized aggressively.

### 2.2 Preserve-Then-Quantize (PTQ) Pipeline

Given a budget of $b_\text{avg}$ average bits per weight, we solve:

$$
\min_{\{b_l\}} \sum_l \mathbb{E}\!\left[\|\mathbf{W}_l - Q_{b_l}(\mathbf{W}_l)\|_F^2 \cdot \mathbf{S}_l\right]
\quad \text{s.t.} \quad
\frac{1}{L}\sum_{l=1}^{L} b_l = b_\text{avg}
$$

where $Q_{b}(\cdot)$ is the uniform quantizer at $b$ bits and $\mathbf{S}_l$ is the saliency matrix of layer $l$.

We solve this with a simple greedy algorithm: layers are ranked by $\operatorname{tr}(\mathbf{S}_l)$ and assigned bits from highest to lowest.

### 2.3 Outlier Smoothing

Following SmoothQuant, we migrate activation outliers into weights before quantisation. For a linear layer $\mathbf{y} = \mathbf{W}\mathbf{x}$:

$$
\mathbf{y} = \underbrace{(\mathbf{W} \mathbf{\Lambda})}_{\tilde{\mathbf{W}}} \underbrace{(\mathbf{\Lambda}^{-1} \mathbf{x})}_{\tilde{\mathbf{x}}}
$$

where $\Lambda = \operatorname{diag}(\lambda_1, \ldots, \lambda_d)$ with $\lambda_j = \max(|x_j|)^{\alpha}$ and $\alpha \in [0.4, 0.6]$.

## 3. Theoretical Analysis

**Proposition 1** *(Error bound).*
Let $\hat{\mathbf{W}} = Q_b(\mathbf{W})$ be the quantized weight and $\Delta = \mathbf{W} - \hat{\mathbf{W}}$ the quantisation error. Then the change in the pre-softmax logit satisfies:

$$
\bigl\|\mathbf{y} - \hat{\mathbf{y}}\bigr\|_2
  \;\le\; \|\mathbf{x}\|_2 \cdot \|\Delta\|_F
  \;\le\; \|\mathbf{x}\|_2 \cdot \frac{\sqrt{d_\text{out}\, d_\text{in}}}{2^b - 1} \cdot R_{\mathbf{W}}
$$

where $R_{\mathbf{W}} = \max_{ij} |w_{ij}| - \min_{ij} |w_{ij}|$ is the weight range.

By allocating higher bits to layers with large $\operatorname{tr}(\mathbf{S}_l)$, PTQ minimises the *expected* output perturbation.

## 5. Conclusion

PTQ introduces a principled, computationally lightweight approach to mixed-precision post-training quantization. By protecting high-saliency weight subspaces, we recover near-lossless quality at W4A8 precision—enabling LLaMA-3 70B to run on a single 48 GB GPU.
