The prevailing narrative surrounding artificial intelligence often focuses on substitution: the idea that machines will inevitably replace human labour across various domains. However, a more productive and realistic framework is to view AI through the lens of a **partnership**. AI is not a replacement; it is an intelligent collaborator that accelerates our decision-making capabilities.

## The Intelligent Partner

When we collaborate with human colleagues, we delegate tasks based on comparative advantage. We assign data-heavy, repetitive analysis to those best equipped for it, while reserving strategic oversight for those with broader context. Our relationship with AI should follow the same principle.

## Accelerating Decision-Making

Consider a complex optimisation problem in reinforcement learning. The AI can simulate millions of trajectories and suggest a policy that maximises a specified reward function. Yet, it is the human who must define what that reward function *should* be, ensuring it aligns with real-world constraints and ethical standards. 

The AI acts as an accelerator. It reduces the time between formulating a hypothesis and evaluating its consequences. This allows human researchers to explore a wider space of ideas, iterating more rapidly than ever before.

> "The true power of AI lies not in autonomous action, but in its capacity to amplify human intent."

## The Final Key: Human Judgement

No matter how sophisticated the algorithm, the ultimate key to decision-making must remain in human hands. AI systems, particularly those based on deep learning, can exhibit unpredictable behaviour when faced with out-of-distribution data. They lack the intrinsic common sense and moral compass that human beings possess.

By defining AI as an *accelerator* rather than a *substitute*, we establish a framework where technology serves to elevate human potential. We move away from the fear of obsolescence and towards a future of unprecedented collaborative achievement.
