## Motivation

Standard reinforcement learning algorithms frequently demonstrate catastrophic policy degradation when exposed to non-stationary or continuously varying reward scalings. This inherent fragility presents a profound impediment to their deployment in real-world environments, which are intrinsically characterised by stochasticity and adversarial perturbations. This research endeavours to systematically dissect these vulnerabilities and engineer a principled mathematical framework to enforce policy stabilisation.

## Problem Formulation

We formalise the environment as a Markov Decision Process (MDP) modulated by a non-stationary external reward scaling factor, denoted as $\alpha_t \in (0, 1]$. The primary objective is to cultivate a robust policy, $\pi_\theta$, that maximises the expected return whilst avoiding catastrophic collapse under extreme perturbations of $\alpha_t$. The empirical validation is conducted within the rigorous continuous control suite provided by MuJoCo.

## Methodology

Our proposed architecture integrates Bayesian optimisation to dynamically regulate the exploration temperature parameter within entropy-regularised policy gradients, specifically the Soft Actor-Critic (SAC) algorithm. By conceptualising the environmental reward scale as a continuous contextual variable, we construct a surrogate Gaussian Process model—leveraging GPyTorch for real-time temperature optimisation—to predict and apply the theoretically optimal temperature schedules dynamically.

## Experimental Protocol

- **Environments:** HalfCheetah-v4 and Ant-v4 (MuJoCo).
- **Baselines:** Standard Soft Actor-Critic (SAC), Proximal Policy Optimisation (PPO) with reward clipping.
- **Evaluation Metric:** Average return across evaluation episodes, measured under highly volatile $\alpha_t$ distributions.

## Key Results

### Scenario 1: Sinusoidal Reward Drift

Under conditions of periodic and sinusoidal reward fluctuations, the Bayesian-SAC formulation demonstrated exemplary adaptability. The dynamically calibrated Bayesian temperature successfully accommodated the underlying uncertainty, thereby preserving structural policy stability. Empirical analysis derived from `log_sinusoidal.csv` indicates that the proposed Bayesian-SAC yielded an approximately **77.8% reduction in return variance** compared to the standard SAC baseline.

### Scenario 2: Abrupt Reward Collapse

In the event of an abrupt reward system collapse, the model exhibited principled uncertainty handling. A rapid temperature spike during the reward collapse effectively prevented catastrophic policy failure by instantly amplifying the exploration mechanism. Analysis of `log_collapse.csv` reveals that the variance during the collapse phase was reduced by approximately **40%**, substantiating the algorithm's resilience.

## Concluding Insights

- **Temperature scaling exhibits profound sensitivity:** Static entropy regularisation coefficients are fundamentally ill-equipped to adapt to non-stationary, stochastic reward distributions.
- **Bayesian priors are indispensable:** The integration of a well-calibrated prior expedites the dynamic adjustment of the temperature parameter, thereby circumventing premature convergence to sub-optimal, deterministic policies.
