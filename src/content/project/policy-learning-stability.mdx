## Motivation

Standard reinforcement learning algorithms often exhibit significant instability when subject to continuous variations in reward scaling. This fragility impedes their deployment in real-world scenarios where reward functions are non-stationary or subject to adversarial perturbations. This project aims to systematically investigate and mitigate this instability.

## Problem Setting

We formalise the environment as a Markov Decision Process (MDP) subject to an external reward scaling factor $\alpha_t \in (0, 1]$. The objective is to learn a robust policy $\pi_\theta$ that maximises expected return without collapsing under extreme values of $\alpha_t$, specifically within the MuJoCo continuous control suite.

## Method

Our approach integrates Bayesian optimisation to dynamically adjust the exploration temperature parameter in entropy-regularised policy gradients (e.g., SAC). By treating the reward scale as a contextual variable, we construct a surrogate model—**GPyTorch 기반의 가우시안 프로세스를 활용한 실시간 온도 최적화** (Real-time temperature optimisation using GPyTorch-based Gaussian Processes)—to predict optimal temperature schedules.

## Experiment Setup

- **Environments:** HalfCheetah-v4 and Ant-v4 (MuJoCo).
- **Baselines:** Standard SAC, PPO with reward clipping.
- **Evaluation Metric:** Average return over evaluation episodes under varying $\alpha_t$ regimes.

## Key Lessons Learned

- **Temperature scaling is highly sensitive:** Static entropy coefficients fail to adapt to non-stationary reward distributions.
- **Bayesian priors matter:** A well-calibrated prior significantly accelerates the adaptation of the temperature parameter, preventing early convergence to sub-optimal policies.
